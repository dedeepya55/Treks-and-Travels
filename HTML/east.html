EASTERN INDIA

Eastern India is a diverse region with a rich tapestry of cultural and natural attractions. West Bengal features Kolkata’s colonial architecture and vibrant culture, along with Darjeeling's stunning Himalayan views and tea gardens, and the Sunderbans National Park's unique mangrove forests and Bengal tigers. Odisha is known for Bhubaneswar’s ancient temples, Puri’s Jagannath Temple and beautiful beaches, and Konark’s Sun Temple with its intricate stone carvings.

Adventures

Kundli Hill in Odisha is a prime destination for paragliding enthusiasts, offering breathtaking aerial views of the lush landscapes and rolling hills. The hill's ideal wind conditions and scenic beauty make it a popular spot for adventure seekers looking to experience the thrill of flight. Meanwhile, Daringbadi, often referred to as the "Kashmir of Odisha," is perfect for camping amidst its pine forests and coffee plantations. The serene environment, combined with the cool climate and picturesque surroundings, provides a memorable camping experience for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Powerfull Temples

Eastern India is home to some of the most powerful and revered temples, each steeped in history and spirituality. The Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, stands as a paramount symbol of devotion and architectural grandeur. Built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva, this temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is a major pilgrimage site, particularly during the annual Rath Yatra festival, where the deities Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are placed on giant chariots and pulled through the streets by thousands of devotees..

Wild Wonders Await: Eastern India's Untamed Beauty

East India, encompassing states like West Bengal, Odisha, Jharkhand, and Bihar, is a region rich in biodiversity, offering a unique blend of ecosystems ranging from lush forests and sprawling grasslands to coastal wetlands and riverine habitats. The Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest shared by India and Bangladesh, is home to the iconic Bengal tiger, estuarine crocodiles, and a myriad of bird species.

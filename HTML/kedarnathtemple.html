OVERVIEW
Kedarnath Temple, nestled in the Garhwal Himalayan range near the Mandakini River in Uttarakhand, is one of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters, it is the highest among the twelve Jyotirlingas and a significant part of the Char Dham pilgrimage. The temple, believed to have been built by the Pandavas and revived by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century, is renowned for its stunning backdrop of snow-clad peaks and its spiritual ambiance. Despite its remote and challenging location, thousands of devotees trek to Kedarnath every year, drawn by their faith and the temple's serene and majestic surroundings.
